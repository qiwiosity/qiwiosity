# Qiwiosity — logo brief & image-gen prompts

A design brief and a set of prompts for generating logo concepts in
Midjourney, DALL-E, Ideogram, or Stable Diffusion. Iterate freely.

## What the name is actually saying

Three layers, read from left to right:

- **Q** (not K). A stylised letterform that signals "we're doing something different."
  In the existing mark, it's a speech bubble — the audio-storytelling engine.
- **IWI** in the middle. The Māori word for tribe / people. Grounds the app in
  Aotearoa and te ao Māori. Evoked with restraint and respect, not appropriated.
- **-OSITY**. From *curiosity*. The emotion the app exists to spark —
  "what's that over there? I wonder…"

The feeling the logo should carry: **wonder triggered by being somewhere
special, and being told a good story about it, by someone who belongs there.**

## Hard constraints

- Works as an app icon from 32×32 px up to 1024×1024 px.
- Works as a horizontal wordmark in a website header.
- Readable on light and dark backgrounds.
- Feels rooted in Aotearoa without clichéd kiwi-bird-and-silver-fern pile-up.
- Evokes te ao Māori **respectfully**. Avoid direct copies of tā moko,
  specific kōwhaiwhai patterns, or anything sacred (tapu). Koru-inspired
  spirals are widely used but should still be handled with care and good
  taste.
- Audio / speech-bubble DNA should be visible.

## Primary prompt — paste into your image generator

```
Minimalist vector logo for a travel app called "Qiwiosity" — a New Zealand
audio travel companion. The letter Q is the hero element, rendered as a
soft speech bubble. The tail of the Q curls into a subtle koru-inspired
spiral that also reads as a winding road through a landscape. Inside the
Q's counter, a simple silhouette of a New Zealand landform — a mountain
peak with a coastline beneath — rendered in gentle geometry. Palette:
deep teal, native-bush green, warm coastal sand, soft sky blue, off-white.
Clean, modern, friendly, confident. Designed to work as an app icon at
small sizes. Flat vector, medium-weight lines, rounded corners, high
legibility. No text, no kitsch, no kiwi birds, no fern leaves.
```

## Variation prompts

Run the primary prompt first, then rotate one element per batch so
the results diverge meaningfully rather than looking like the same
idea over and over.

### Swap the landscape element inside the Q

Replace the mountain + coastline phrase with one of these:

- *a braided river curling from top to bottom*
- *twin islands abstracted as soft overlapping shapes*
- *a geothermal plume rising from the base*
- *a single fern frond unfurling*
- *a pounamu-inspired teardrop curve*
- *a constellation suggesting the Southern Cross*
- *a path of three dots ending in a map pin*

### Wordmark lockup

```
Horizontal wordmark for "Qiwiosity". The Q extra-prominent, illustrated
as a rounded speech bubble with a koru-inspired tail. Lowercase
"iwiosity" next to it in a warm geometric sans-serif, with "iwi" given
subtle emphasis through a slightly different weight or a soft underline
so the Q-IWI-osity reading reveals itself. Deep teal on off-white.
Clean, friendly, modern — think Duolingo / Airbnb / Headspace tier
of approachability. No illustrations beside the wordmark.
```

### App-icon-only monogram

```
App icon mark: a single stylised letter Q, rendered as a speech bubble
with a koru-inspired tail. The counter of the Q contains a minimalist
Aotearoa motif. Designed for a squircle app icon background at
1024×1024. Deep teal mark on a warm cream background, or a reversed
white-on-teal version. High contrast, legible at 32×32. Flat vector.
```

### "Map pin meets speech bubble" direction

```
Logo mark where a map pin and a speech bubble share the same silhouette,
forming a stylised letter Q. The pointed tail of the map pin doubles as
the descender of the Q and the tail of the speech bubble. Palette: deep
teal and warm coral accent. Minimalist flat vector, friendly, modern.
Designed as an app icon.
```

### "Quiet dawn" direction (different mood)

```
Logo for a travel storytelling app "Qiwiosity". The Q is soft, almost
hand-drawn, with a speech-bubble tail that melts into a koru spiral.
Inside the counter, a sunrise over a low mountain ridge. Muted, warm,
early-morning palette: soft peach, sage green, pale teal, cream.
Evokes a quiet moment on a road trip. Slightly illustrative rather
than sharp-edged vector.
```

## Style-direction modifiers

Pick **one** per generation and append to the prompt. Mixing them gives
muddy results.

- `, in the style of a modern travel brand like Airbnb or Duolingo`
- `, reminiscent of mid-century modernist logo design, Paul Rand influence`
- `, slightly hand-drawn and warm, like a Tracks Magazine or Monocle illustration`
- `, geometric and architectural, flat shapes, limited palette`
- `, in the spirit of New Zealand contemporary designers like Dick Frizzell or Johnson Witehira` (use carefully and only for reference mood, not to copy)

## Palette suggestions

- **Deep teal** (#0E5E6F) — primary, rooted, trustworthy, a nod to NZ coastline
- **Native-bush green** (#3C6E47) — secondary, earthed, evergreen
- **Warm coastal sand** (#E8D6B3) — background, warmth
- **Soft sky blue** (#BFE0E6) — accents, lightness
- **Pounamu green** (#4A7C59) — optional accent; evokes greenstone without copying it

Avoid:

- Silver-fern black-and-silver (too All Blacks, too sports).
- Union Jack blue + red (too colonial, too 1970s tourism poster).
- Aggressively saturated electric teal (too tech-bro, too Headspace).

## Before you commit to any direction

1. **Generate 20–30 variations** across the prompts above.
   Pick your 3–5 favourites.
2. **Ask five people who aren't you** what the app does, shown only the logo.
   If most say "travel + audio + New Zealand", you're there.
3. **Commission a proper designer** for the final version once you know the
   direction — NZ has excellent designers. AI-generated logos are great for
   exploration but rarely ship at production quality without a human
   reworking the curves, kerning, and edge cases (favicon, monochrome, dark
   mode, merchandise).

## The current mark

The mark you already have (Q as speech bubble, green gradient, landscape backdrop)
is a strong starting point. Worth running the prompts above partly to see what
else exists, and partly to give a designer options when you commission the
polished final. Keep the current mark as your working logo for now — it's
absolutely good enough for the Week 1 prototype, the landing page, and the
closed beta.

# Revision 2 — bolder, more minimal, audio-first

The previous iterations landed somewhere close to generic NZ tourism
(mountain + water + beach in a circle), lost the audio/storytelling meaning,
and carried too much internal detail to scale cleanly.

New direction: **the letter Q is a speech bubble.** That's the whole idea.
No landscape, no internal scene, no fern. One bold shape, one or two colours,
no decoration. Think Airbnb's *bélo*, Threads' @, Headspace's orange dot,
Mastodon's elephant silhouette — a single memorable idea delivered at
maximum clarity.

## New constraints

- **One bold shape.** Single idea. No scene-building.
- **One or two colours max.** Flat fill, no gradients, no textures.
- **Thick, confident line weight** OR **solid fill with negative-space
  detail.** Either way — no thin lines, no fine features.
- **Must read at 16×16 px.** If you squint and can't see what it is, it's
  failed.
- **Q and speech bubble are the same element.** The viewer shouldn't think
  "that's a Q with a speech bubble next to it" — they should think "that's
  a Q that is a speech bubble."

## New primary prompt

```
Minimalist app logo: the letter Q rendered as a bold speech bubble.
Single flat colour, thick uniform stroke, no internal detail, no
landscape, no illustration. The descender of the Q is unmistakably
the tail of a speech bubble, not decorative. Deep teal on off-white.
Feels confident, iconic, app-icon-first. Inspired by the simplicity
of Airbnb's bélo, Threads, and Headspace. Designed to be instantly
recognisable at 32x32 pixels. Flat vector, centred composition,
generous whitespace, no text.
```

## Variation prompts — four directions to explore in parallel

### A. Sound-waves direction (keeps "audio" loud and clear)

```
Minimalist logo: the letter Q as a rounded speech bubble. Three small,
concentric arcs radiate outward from the tail of the Q, suggesting
sound travelling outward. Single flat deep-teal colour on off-white.
Thick, confident line work. No internal detail. Iconic, minimal,
app-icon-ready. Inspired by the Airbnb bélo for simplicity.
```

### B. Negative-space direction (Q and bubble are one mark)

```
Minimalist mark: a bold filled circle forms the letter Q. The counter
(the inside cut-out) is shaped as a speech bubble — a rounded form
with a small tail pointing down-right. Negative-space treatment
makes the Q and the speech bubble the same shape. Single deep-teal
fill on off-white. No text, no illustration, no detail. Iconic, confident,
scales cleanly to 16x16. Inspired by the Mastodon and Threads marks.
```

### C. Q-as-quote direction (storytelling tone, very minimal)

```
Logo mark: the letter Q where the curl of the descender reads as a
large bold quotation mark, and the overall silhouette reads as a speech
bubble. Thick uniform stroke, single colour (deep teal) on off-white.
No internal fill. Evokes storytelling and spoken word. Minimal, iconic,
app-icon ready, legible at 16x16.
```

### D. Warm minimalism (softer, friendlier, Duolingo/Headspace tier)

```
Minimalist logo for a travel audio app. The letter Q as a rounded,
friendly speech bubble. Warm coral or burnt-orange solid fill on a
warm off-white background. Soft rounded edges, slightly imperfect
circle so it feels crafted rather than corporate. No internal detail,
no text, no illustration. Inspired by Headspace's playful minimalism.
Iconic, warm, inviting, friendly.
```

## Palette options for Revision 2

Pick **one** palette per batch — don't let the AI mix them.

- **Deep teal on cream.** #0E5E6F on #F6F0E1. Primary recommendation —
  confident, NZ-coastal, serious-but-warm.
- **Warm coral on cream.** #E27D60 on #F6F0E1. Friendlier, more Headspace.
- **Near-black on cream.** #1A1A1A on #F6F0E1. Most editorial, most timeless.
- **Pounamu green on stone.** #4A7C59 on #EDE8E0. Most Aotearoa-rooted.

## What to run first

1. Run the **primary prompt** in deep teal on cream — generate 20 variations.
2. Run the **negative-space** variation — generate 20 more. This is the one
   most likely to produce something Airbnb-bélo iconic.
3. Run the **sound-waves** variation if you want to keep "audio" explicit
   in the mark.
4. Skip the warm-minimal variation unless the first three feel too serious.

Pick your favourite 3, ignore the rest.

## How to tell if Revision 2 is working

Ask yourself (or a friend) about a candidate mark with no context:

- **Can you read it as a letter Q without being told?** If no — it's failed
  as a letterform.
- **Does it look like something could come out of it — words, sound, a
  story?** If no — it's failed as a speech bubble.
- **Would you recognise it as the same mark on a 16×16 favicon as you
  would on a billboard?** If no — it's too complex.

If all three land, you have your logo.
