# Tolaga Bay Wharf — headline (22s)

Six hundred and sixty metres of reinforced concrete walking out into the Pacific — the longest wharf in the southern hemisphere. It took three years to build in the 1920s. Its last ship called in 1966. It's still there. You can walk every inch of it.

- 660 m long. ✅ NZ History / Te Ara.

- Last ship Kopara, 1966. ✅ NZ History.
