# Napier–Taupō Road (SH5) — headline (28s)

You're about to drive one of New Zealand's most storied pieces of tarseal. Once a Māori track. Then, from 1872, a two-day Cobb & Co coach run. Before World War Two, truck drivers stashed spare gearboxes along the route because the climbs broke them. In 2023, Cyclone Gabrielle closed it for five weeks. Enjoy it. It's earned its reputation.

---

**Voice direction:** conversational, faintly respectful. Small grin on "stashed spare gearboxes." Pause before "It's earned its reputation."

**Runtime target:** 26–32 seconds.

**Trigger suggestion:** fire this as an intro when drivers turn onto SH5 at the Bay View junction (from Napier) or at the Wairakei roundabout (from Taupō). Large trigger radius — this is a corridor piece, not a point.

**Fact-check:**
- Original route was a Māori footpath predating European arrival. ✅
- Cobb & Co launched the Napier–Taupō coach service on 1 January 1872. ✅ Te Ara / HB Knowledge Bank.
- Pre-WWII trucks and spare gearboxes at Turangakumu/Titiokura climbs. ✅ Contractor Magazine heritage piece.
- SH5 closed after Cyclone Gabrielle 14 Feb 2023, reopened to traffic 20 March 2023 (~5 weeks). ✅ 1News / NZTA.
