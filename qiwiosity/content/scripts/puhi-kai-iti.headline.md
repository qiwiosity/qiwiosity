# Puhi Kai Iti / Cook Landing Site — headline (25s)

On the ninth of October 1769, two worlds met here for the first time. James Cook's Endeavour landed. A Ngāti Oneone rangatira named Te Maro was shot and killed. Cook left two days later, called it Poverty Bay, and the name stuck for two hundred and fifty years. Tūranganui-a-Kiwa is the name that was always here.

**Fact-check:**
- 9 Oct 1769 landing. ✅ DOC / NZ History.
- Nicholas Young sighted land on 7 Oct from the masthead ("Young Nick"). ✅
- Te Maro of Ngāti Oneone killed on the first day. ✅ NZ History / DOC.
- Nine Māori killed across the first two days. ✅ Wikipedia.
- "Poverty Bay" named by Cook because he couldn't get supplies. ✅
- Now officially "Tūranganui-a-Kiwa / Poverty Bay". ✅ NZGB dual name.
- Puhi Kai Iti is the Māori name for the pā site built by Māia (Horouta waka captain) at Kaiti Beach. ✅
