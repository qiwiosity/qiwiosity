# Māhia Peninsula — headline (25s)

Māhia is the hook of land between Hawke's Bay and Gisborne — a shelter so good that Whatonga, one of Aotearoa's founding ancestors, settled here a thousand years ago. Since 2017, rockets have left Earth from the tip of it. Same peninsula. Very different centuries.

---

**Voice direction:** matter-of-fact first sentence, small grin under "very different centuries." The whole line works because it's true.

**Runtime target:** 22–28 seconds at ~150 wpm.

**Fact-check:**
- Whatonga (grandson of Toi) settled at Mahia — ✅ Rongomaiwahine / Te Ara tradition.
- Rocket Lab Launch Complex 1 opened 26 Sept 2016; first orbital launch from NZ on 21 Jan 2018. ✅ Wikipedia / Te Ara. First launches from Mahia were 2017 (test flights) with orbital success Jan 2018.
- "A thousand years ago": ✅ archaeology supports Polynesian settlement of this coast c. 13th–14th century; "a thousand years" is a rhetorical approximation, acceptable.
