# Waipawa — headline (22s)

Waipawa was founded in 1860 as "Abbottsford", on a ford across the river. The settlers kept trying. The name Waipawa kept winning. A hundred and sixty years later, it's one of the quietest, most intact colonial main streets in New Zealand — and, long before that, a major Ngāti Kahungunu kainga.

---

**Voice direction:** dry, amused by "the settlers kept trying." Let "a hundred and sixty years later" breathe. End with respect.

**Runtime target:** 20–24 seconds at ~150 wpm.

**Fact-check:**
- Founded 1860 by Frederick Abbott (runholder). ✅ Te Ara + Wikipedia.
- Originally named Abbottsford; Māori name Waipawa preferred by settlers themselves. ✅
- Pre-European: the Waipawa / Tukituki plains were major Ngāti Kahungunu occupation — particularly Ngāi Toroiwaho and Ngāti Whatuiāpiti hapū. ✅ Kahungunu iwi materials.
