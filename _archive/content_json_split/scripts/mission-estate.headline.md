# Mission Estate — headline (22s)

This is New Zealand's oldest winery. French Marist missionaries landed in Hawke's Bay in 1851 with vines in their luggage. They've been making wine here ever since — through two relocations, one flood, and the 1931 earthquake, which killed two priests and seven students in this building's chapel.

---

**Fact-check:**
- 1851 establishment, French Marists, Pakowhai site. ✅ Mission Estate / Wikipedia.
- Two priests and seven students killed in the stone chapel, 1931 earthquake. ✅ Wikipedia.
- Moved from Pakowhai to Meeanee (1858), then to Greenmeadows (1909) after a flood. Final move — the building was cut into 11 sections and rolled on logs over 5 km in 2 days. ✅ Wikipedia.
- New Zealand's oldest winery. ✅ (Some claim overlap with Babich et al., but 1851 predates them all.)
