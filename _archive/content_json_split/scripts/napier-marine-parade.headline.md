# Marine Parade / Art Deco Napier — headline (20s)

On the third of February, 1931, Napier fell down in two and a half minutes. Two hundred and fifty-six people died. What rose out of the rubble — in thirty months — is the town you're looking at now: the most complete Art Deco seafront in the world.

---

**Voice direction:** steady, matter-of-fact for the first sentence. Soft landing on "thirty months" — the turnaround is the astonishing part.

**Runtime target:** 18–22 seconds at 150 wpm.

**Fact-check:**
- 1931 Hawke's Bay earthquake, magnitude 7.8, 3 Feb, 10:47am. ✅ GNS / Te Ara.
- 256 dead: widely cited figure. Cross-check with MTG Hawke's Bay exhibition plaque.
- "30 months": need confirmation — common claim is that most of the CBD was rebuilt by 1933. Phrasing can survive a 24–36 month range.
- "Most complete Art Deco seafront in the world": Napier Art Deco Trust claim. Frequently echoed; worth citing as Trust's position in long-form.
