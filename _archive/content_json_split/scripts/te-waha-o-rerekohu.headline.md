# Te Waha-o-Rerekohu — headline (22s)

In the grounds of a tiny school at Te Araroa stands what is, almost certainly, the largest and oldest pōhutukawa tree on earth. Six hundred years old. A branch span of thirty-seven metres. Its name means "the mouth of Rerekohu".

---

**Voice direction:** hushed, the way you'd describe a church. Let "thirty-seven metres" land.

**Runtime target:** 20–24 seconds.

**Fact-check:**
- Largest and oldest pōhutukawa in New Zealand — ✅ widely published (Te Ara, iNaturalist, etc.).
- Age: 600+ years. ✅ Estimates cluster around 600, with one study dating it to ~1421 ± 200 years.
- Branch span: over 37 m when measured in 1950. ✅ Te Ara.
- Located at Te Araroa Area School grounds. ✅ Publicly accessible.
- Name "Te Waha o Rerekohu" = "the mouth of Rerekohu" — a historical rangatira of the area. ✅
