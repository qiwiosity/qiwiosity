# Mōhaka Viaduct — headline (22s)

You're looking at the tallest railway viaduct in Australasia — ninety-five metres up, two hundred and seventy-seven metres long. Started in 1930. Halted by the Depression, battered by the 1931 earthquake, and finished anyway in 1937. It still carries trains today.

---

**Voice direction:** just the numbers, steady and proud. Drop a half-beat before "and finished anyway."

**Runtime target:** 20–24 seconds at ~150 wpm.

**Fact-check:**
- 95 m tall, 276.8 m long. ✅ Wikipedia / Engineering New Zealand / Structurae.
- Tallest in Australasia. ✅
- Construction 1930–1937, with a ~5 year pause for the Depression (work abandoned 1931, resumed 1936). ✅ Wikipedia.
- 1931 Hawke's Bay earthquake (3 Feb) caused major damage to the railway line and associated works. ✅ Wikipedia.
- Opened July 1937. ✅ Wikipedia.
- Heritage NZ Category 1 listing no. 4418. ✅
- Designed by PWD engineers John Lelliot Cull and William Langston Newnham. ✅
