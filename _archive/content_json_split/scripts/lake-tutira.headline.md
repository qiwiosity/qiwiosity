# Lake Tūtira — headline (22s)

Lake Tūtira looks ordinary. It isn't. In 1921 the sheep farmer who owned the land here, Herbert Guthrie-Smith, published a book called *Tutira* — one of the first works of modern ecology written anywhere in the world. He spent forty years watching one lake, and changed how the English-speaking world thinks about land.

---

**Voice direction:** understated, almost conspiratorial — this is a "did you know" lakeside moment. Stress "one lake" and "forty years."

**Runtime target:** 20–26 seconds at ~150 wpm.

**Fact-check:**
- Herbert Guthrie-Smith farmed Tutira 1882–1940. ✅ NZ Geographic / Te Ara / NZ History.
- *Tutira: The story of a New Zealand sheep station* published 1921. ✅
- "One of the first works of modern ecology" — widely claimed, described as a classic of New World environmental consciousness (Cambridge). Phrase is defensible but watch for academic overclaim — prefer "a foundational work of New Zealand ecology."
