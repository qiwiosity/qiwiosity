# Bluff Hill / Mataruahou — standard (90–120s)

The hill you're on has a Māori name — Mataruahou — and a job. For centuries it was the pā headland that watched the coast. In 1931 it did something nobody was expecting: it grew.

When the earthquake struck on the third of February, the Pacific Plate and the Australian Plate shifted violently along the fault line that runs under Hawke's Bay. Mataruahou was pushed up by roughly two metres. At the same time, the Ahuriri Lagoon — a vast, shallow inland sea that stretched from right under your feet out to the north — was drained. Tens of square kilometres of seabed dried out in minutes. The Māori fishing villages along the lagoon edge suddenly found themselves high and dry. The lagoon never came back.

From where you're standing, look north. That flat land — the port, the industrial zone, Napier Airport, the suburbs of Onekawa and Pirimai — all of it used to be water. Napier didn't just rebuild itself after the quake; it physically doubled its footprint. The port below expanded, the airport was eventually built on reclaimed land, and a town that had been squeezed onto two small hills suddenly had room to breathe.

The viewpoint is best at sunset or in early morning — the light comes in off the Pacific and the whole bay opens up. On a clear day you can see Mahia Peninsula to the north, Cape Kidnappers to the south, and the long curve of the coastline in between. That curve is the bite of the fish — Te Matau-a-Māui, the fish hook.

**Voice direction:** geography and story in equal measure. Slow on the phrase "it grew". Pick up again on the "look north" cue — it's a direct instruction to the listener.

**Runtime target:** 100–115 seconds at ~150 wpm.

**Fact-check:**
- Mataruahou: correct Māori name for Bluff Hill. ✅ Napier City Council signage, Te Ara.
- "Pacific Plate and Australian Plate" — the 1931 rupture was on the North Island Dextral Fault Belt; the plate-boundary framing is simplified but acceptable for a general-audience script.
- 2m uplift: ✅ commonly cited. GNS lists 1.8–2.7m.
- Lagoon drainage: ✅ core fact of the quake's geography.
- Airport on reclaimed land: ✅ Hawke's Bay Airport history.
- "Te Matau-a-Māui": ✅ accepted Māori name for the bay.
