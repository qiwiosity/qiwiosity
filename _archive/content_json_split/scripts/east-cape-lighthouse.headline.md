# East Cape Lighthouse — headline (22s)

Seven hundred steps up the hill, the easternmost lighthouse on earth. On a clear morning, this is the first place on the planet the sun hits. Or rather — one of a few. Kiribati moved the dateline in 1995 and technically beat us. Geographically, we're still first.

---

**Voice direction:** dry Kiwi accuracy. The Kiribati gag should land with a small shrug — we're honest about it.

**Runtime target:** 22–26 seconds.

**Fact-check:**
- Easternmost lighthouse in the world. ✅ Maritime NZ.
- 700 steps to the lighthouse from the car park. ✅ commonly cited — verify on AA / DOC signage (varies between 700 and 800 in published sources). "Roughly 700" is safer.
- Kiribati moved the dateline in 1995 to put the whole country east of it — ✅ yes. That made Caroline Island (Millennium Island) the first inhabited place to see each new day.
- East Cape still holds the first-sunrise honour geographically due to earth curvature, if you discount the dateline adjustment.
- Lighthouse history: original 1900 on East Island; moved to the mainland in 1922, automated 1985. ✅ Maritime NZ / Wikipedia.
