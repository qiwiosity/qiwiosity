# Bluff Hill / Mataruahou — headline (20s)

The hill you're standing on grew in 1931. The earthquake pushed Mataruahou up by about two metres — and drained the lagoon where Napier airport now sits. Everything flat you can see to the north used to be under water.

---

**Voice direction:** conversational. The "grew" framing is the hook — let it land.

**Runtime target:** 16–20 seconds at ~150 wpm.

**Fact-check:**
- 1931 uplift: ~1.8–2.7m along coastal Napier. ✅ GNS Science.
- 3,500 hectares of new land exposed — widely cited. Te Ara uses "about 40 square kilometres" (≈ 4,000ha). Use "thousands of hectares" if unsure.
- Airport built on reclaimed lagoon: ✅ correct.
